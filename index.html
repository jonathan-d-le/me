About

An engineer's precision, an immigrant's drive.

I'm a Senior Supplier Quality Engineer in medical devices, where the products end up inside someone's body — so "good enough" means whether a device performs safely for a real patient. That bar shapes how I work.

I've sat on both sides of the table: as a manufacturing engineer making the parts, and as the quality engineer holding suppliers accountable for them. That makes me practical about what's actually achievable on a production floor, and stubborn about the things that can't be compromised.

My approach comes with a hustle I come by honestly — a get-it-done energy rooted in my family's story. But my field rewards the opposite instinct too: patience, precision, and the discipline to slow down and get it exactly right. Learning to hold both at once is the most useful thing I've figured out.

I read, write, and speak both English and Vietnamese fluently, and my heritage shapes how I build trust across cultures and teams. As more medical-device and electronics manufacturing scales across the US and Asia-Pacific, that bridge keeps proving useful.

Experience

A climb through quality & manufacturing.

Tap any role to expand. Internal program and supplier names have been generalized.

Lead supplier quality and compliance for medical-device production under FDA 21 CFR Part 820 and ISO 13485.

  • Lead supplier quality across multiple suppliers and contract manufacturers, ensuring sustained regulatory compliance.
  • Led the supplier-quality response to a critical foreign-material investigation — driving corrective actions, partnering directly with suppliers, and supporting on-site lab work; recognized at the VP level and helped lift a finished-good hold to enable timely shipment.
  • Drove the operating unit's first full product through PPAP, establishing tight supplier controls while managing a contract manufacturer through significant challenges.
  • Lead daily process-validation (OQ/PQ) meetings with a contract manufacturer, coaching the supplier toward faster, higher-quality results.
  • Caught and corrected an error in a tolerance-interval analysis during PPAP review using applied statistics, then documented the learnings as reusable guidance for the team.
  • Closed high-priority supplier change orders in record turnaround to clear backorders and protect shipment schedules.

Owned supplier quality for the components and materials feeding medical-device production.

  • Served as supplier quality engineer on a ~2-year manufacturing transfer, closing all PPAP deliverables and passing the external certification audit with zero findings — a seamless transfer by company standards.
  • Helped design a transfer strategy that avoided an estimated ~$500K in projected costs by using a business-process approach instead of a costly IT build; supported a cross-functional Kaizen and risk-based gap closure.
  • Supported a global regulatory launch, helping release 65+ product GTINs through implementation and a legal manufacturing change — coordinating across time zones with an overseas contract manufacturer.
  • Devised a creative overpackaging solution that prevented shipping damage while avoiding expensive verification testing and new part numbers.
  • Traveled on-site to international contract manufacturers, often on short notice, to support builds, supplier readiness, and design-verification closure.
  • Created supplier management-of-change questionnaire forms adopted across the team; mentored colleagues on inspection methods.

Hands-on quality engineering in a regulated V&V environment.

  • Conducted failure analysis on returned neuro-implantable products and developed test methods to validate the analysis procedures.
  • Executed software installation, verification, and equipment validation for compliant introduction to technicians.
  • Built and executed qualification protocols and work instructions; validated quality instruments for attribute and variable characteristics of implantable devices.

Learned quality from the build side — making the parts, not just auditing them.

  • Generated and executed IQ/OQ/PQ for new equipment and processes per 21 CFR Part 820 and ISO 13485.
  • Designed production-aid devices and fixtures that reduced scrap and increased line productivity.
  • Operated extruders and analyzed resin moisture to maintain material quality; held tight tolerances in precision cutting.
  • Generated and routed Engineering Change Notifications (ECNs) for controlled documentation.

The work that built the character behind the engineering.

  • Welder & fabrication: operated welding and grinding equipment safely and precisely; coordinated with vendors and metal suppliers to reduce lead time — first exposure to the supplier relationships I now manage.
  • STEM mentoring & tutoring: led individualized sessions that improved student test scores by an average of 60%, building the patience, listening, and clear-communication skills behind every good supplier conversation.

Heritage · Việt Nam

Where it started — and where I'm headed.

My parents left Vietnam so their children could build a different life. That sacrifice is the engine behind how I work — a drive to make it mean something, balanced by the patience and care my field demands.

I bring my culture into everything I do. And one day, I hope to bring everything I've built back home — to help others achieve great things in the place my family once had to leave.

Ba mẹ tôi rời Việt Nam để các con có một cuộc đời khác. Sự hy sinh ấy là động lực cho cách tôi làm việc — một khát vọng để điều đó trở nên có ý nghĩa, cân bằng với sự kiên nhẫn và cẩn trọng mà nghề của tôi đòi hỏi.

Tôi mang văn hóa của mình vào mọi việc tôi làm. Và một ngày nào đó, tôi mong được mang tất cả những gì mình gây dựng trở về quê hương — để giúp những người khác làm nên những điều lớn lao ngay tại nơi gia đình tôi từng phải rời xa.
